The Orbit

If I tried to list the reasons, the sky would run out of stars.

I have said “I love you” many times, but words will never carry the full weight of what that means. You might think my feelings are fleeting sparks, exaggerations that fade with time, but I feel as though I have loved you forever, even if it has only been three years. And I know that I will continue to love you endlessly.

My love isn’t a spark, Zarin. It’s a gravity that keeps me in your orbit no matter how far you drift, no matter if you look back at me or not. You have written of the ache of departures, of people fading when the moment passes. But I am not a passing moment, I am gravity, and gravity stays.

I haven't just fallen for you. I have fallen for the details you probably think go unnoticed. I have fallen for the void in your eyes, not an empty darkness, but a depth I want to fall into and never stop falling. I have fallen for the way you talk. I notice the way your voice lands on words and the pauses you choose. I have fallen for the way you laugh.

I have fallen for the way you eat ice-cream (I saw you that day, and honestly my heart melted faster than the ice cream ever could). I have kept every sticker you have made because they are little pieces of your mind.

I worry about you. I wonder if you have had breakfast, if sleep has been kind to you, if dinner was skipped while your thoughts wandered into shadows. In your poems, I see the tenderness of someone who notices everything. I want you to know that I notice too, and I try to notice everything I can. Not to be creepy, but because every small detail makes the picture of You more beautiful. I notice the pauses in your voice, the way your handwriting breathes across a page, the way your poems carry both wound and healing in the same line.

I sat beside you during the essay competition, and I couldn't stop admiring the perfection of your handwriting. I couldn't stop watching the way your handwriting flowed across the page. It wasn't just words on paper, but the way your thoughts take shape. The curves, the spaces... it felt like watching you create an art with ink. I love the way you leave a trace of yourself in everything you touch.

Your poems tell me you feel imperfect, that chaos lives inside you. I know I may not be the person you want to fill that void with, and I know that I have my own flaws, and I cannot fix everything. I know I cannot rewrite your past or compete with anyone who once held your heart. I won’t try to fill a void with promises I can’t keep. Instead, I will stand beside it like a lantern in the dark - steady, gentle and warm. I will be the person who doesn’t rush your healing, who doesn’t demand certainty, who doesn’t measure love by possession. If you believe nothing lasts, I will let time be my answer. Showing up, staying kind, and choosing you in ways that are quiet and consistent.

I don’t love you despite your imperfections, I love you because of them. Your cracks let me see the living person underneath. I see the beauty in your shadows, and I love you through them. I love you because you are You. The universe seems to stop when you are near. I fell for you because every glimpse felt like truth. Your poems, your stickers, your handwriting, your laugh, your eyes each one is a doorway, and every doorway leads to you.

You can push me away, you can believe that nothing lasts, but I am not going anywhere. I am not asking for you to be perfect. I am just asking you to know that I love you infinitely. You are the most important person in my universe, and I will keep choosing you, in every meal you skip, in every sleepless night, in every poem that bleeds shadows into light.

Je t'aime, Zarin.

Oh, and I wanted to mention another thing...

The moon is beautiful, isn’t it? And somehow, it carries more of you than you realize. Your name is where it belongs, written among the stars, resting in the silence of the moon.

By the way, did you notice
there are exactly 138 stars here?
Some numbers are more than numbers...
