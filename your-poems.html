The Art of You

Thoughts on the words you bleed onto paper.

I haven't said this to you directly, but I am a big fan of your writing, and I really mean it. Your poems aren't the generic happy verses people expect. You write about the shadows, the mundane, and the sadness. You reveal places others don't see, the private suffering behind a facade of "nothing's wrong". But that is exactly why they are beautiful. Most people write to escape reality, but you write to confront it.

Reading your poems feels like looking into a mirror you usually keep covered. I see a struggle for perfectionism in your lines. I see the anxiety of being bound by routine, the feeling of a chaos inside while the world thinks nothing is wrong.

It feels like you are searching for a purpose that was lost, or maybe hiding your true self because you think the world won't understand. You write about imperfections as if they are sins, but I need you to know something... Those imperfections make you who you are.

And then there is Anorexic Angel. You gave this poem to me yourself, and I hold that close to my heart.

When I read it, I see more than just the meaning of the poem. I see the symbolism of the Angel, something pure and holy, being destroyed by a law that demands hunger. It feels like a metaphor for starving oneself of happiness or authenticity just to fit a mold. The Angel is thrown from heaven, labeled as filthy and small because it couldn't keep up with the divine compulsion to be perfect.

It paints a picture of someone trying so hard to be what they think they should be, that they end up hurting the beautiful person they are.

To me, your imperfections aren't flaws, they are the brushstrokes that make you real. I don't love the mask; I love the person underneath who feels everything so deeply.

I love you because you are you. You, exactly as you are, are my definition of perfect, and I hope you never forget that.

- Yours
